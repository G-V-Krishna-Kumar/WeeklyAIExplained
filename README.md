# WeeklyAIExplained

WeeklyAIExplained is a personal learning repository where I document my journey of understanding Artificial Intelligence — from mathematical foundations to research papers and practical implementations.

## About

This started with a simple goal: to read and explain one AI research paper each week.

Over time, I realized that understanding these papers properly requires a stronger grasp of the underlying mathematics and the tools used in practice. Because of that, this repository has gradually evolved into a broader learning space that reflects how I am approaching AI now.

---

## Structure

The repository is organized into four main parts:

### Papers
This section contains explanations of AI research papers across areas like NLP, speech, and (going forward) computer vision.

The focus is on understanding:
- the main idea behind the paper  
- how the model works   

---

### Mathematical Foundations
This includes the mathematical concepts required for AI and ML, such as:
- Linear Algebra  
- Probability and Statistics  
- Calculus  

The goal here is to build intuition, not just follow formulas. Explanations are kept simple, and small code examples are added where helpful.

---

### Core Libraries
This section is for learning commonly used Python libraries like NumPy, Pandas, and PyTorch.

Instead of covering everything, I focus on:
- important functions  
- writing examples from scratch  
- understanding how they are used in ML contexts  

---

### Projects
This section will include small implementations and experiments to apply what I learn from papers, math, and libraries.

It is currently minimal and will be expanded over time.
